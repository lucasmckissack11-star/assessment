Tracen Academy

You arrive at Tracen Academy. You see all the racers that you adored on TV, wondering if you will ever be like them. The soft wind presses against you as you stand outside the front gate. You feel a strong determination and enter without a worry. As you stroll inside a thought strikes you. Who will you look up to?

You pursue the ultimate runaway: Silence Suzuka. She dominated all her races by pushing ahead at the start and keeping her lead the whole race. She is widely known and traveled to the USA to race after dominating Japan. A perfect pick for you, yet many other people have made this perfect pick and not had a successful career, so make sure to work hard.

You pursue the unstoppable force: Symboli Rudolf. She won almost every major race in Japan, famous for her incredible speed and stamina that left rivals behind. Known as a legend of Japanese racing, she even faced top competition from abroad. A strong choice for you, but many have picked her and failed to shine, so stay focused and push yourself to the limit.

You pursue the fierce competitor: Maruzensky. Known for her explosive acceleration and relentless fighting spirit, she often dominated races by closing gaps that seemed impossible. Her battles on the track were legendary in Japan, making her a fan favorite for her courage and determination. A strong choice for anyone chasing glory, but many who picked her faced unexpected challenges, so stay sharp and never underestimate the effort required to succeed.

You decide to rely on the support of a close friend. Three stand out among the crowd: Kitasan Black, Curren Chan, and Super Creek. Each offers a different kind of strength. Choosing a friend wisely can shape your journey at Tracen Academy.

You spend the morning getting to know Curren Chan, laughing and talking as she shares her favorite spots around Tracen Academy. She suddenly pulls out her phone, eyes sparkling with excitement. 'Let’s post a photo together!' she suggests, eager to share your new friendship with everyone. It could be fun and boost your popularity—but it might also bring more attention than you want. Will you join her and post together, or quietly ignore the social media idea and stay out of the spotlight?

Your first post together goes live, showing you and Curren Chan smiling outside the academy. Within a few minutes the notifications start appearing on her phone. The post reaches 120 likes surprisingly quickly, and Curren cheers excitedly beside you. It’s not viral yet, but it feels like the beginning of something bigger..

Over the next week you continue posting moments from training, races, and fun times around the academy. Each post performs better than the last, and people begin recognising you in the hallways. Before you know it, your account has grown by over 2,000 followers. Curren Chan laughs proudly, saying you might become a famous racer and influencer.

During a live stream you suddenly shout “Hachimi!” as a joke with Curren. The clip spreads across social media faster than you expected, with fans reposting it everywhere. By the time you wake up the next morning, your follower count has exploded by 10,000. Suddenly the entire academy seems to know who you are.

A company reaches out with an offer to sponsor you after seeing your rising popularity online. They promise equipment, promotion, and even money if you represent their brand. Once the announcement is posted, your popularity skyrockets even further. Cameras, fans, and interviews begin following you everywhere you go.

With so much time spent filming content and attending sponsor events, your training begins to slip. When the next race arrives, your body isn’t prepared for the pressure. You fall behind early and finish far from the lead. Fans online begin questioning whether you care more about fame than racing.

You decide to stay out of the spotlight and ignore the opportunities around you. While the quiet life feels comfortable, you slowly stop pushing yourself during training. Other racers grow stronger while you remain the same. Eventually your racing career fades before it ever truly begins.

Instead of choosing one path, you carefully balance your time between training and content creation. Early mornings are spent on the track, while evenings are used to connect with fans. People begin to admire not just your personality, but also your dedication and growth. Slowly, you build a reputation as both a serious racer and a rising star.

Despite your sudden popularity, you choose to step away from the spotlight for a while. Instead of streaming or posting constantly, you dedicate yourself completely to training. Rumours begin spreading about how serious you've become on the track. When you return to racing, people are surprised by how much stronger you've grown.

During a stream you boldly claim that you are the best racer at the academy. At first it feels confident, but many viewers interpret it as arrogance. Comments begin flooding in criticizing your attitude. Soon your reputation begins to suffer, and even some fans start turning against you.

Realising your mistake, you upload a heartfelt apology explaining that your words came from excitement rather than arrogance. You thank your supporters and promise to prove yourself through hard work instead of bragging. Some fans remain skeptical, but many appreciate your honesty. Gradually, people begin supporting you again.

After everything that has happened, you decide there are no more shortcuts. Every day becomes focused on refining your technique and pushing your limits. Training sessions become longer and more intense than ever before. Slowly, you begin to feel yourself becoming stronger.

Kitasan Black greets you with a huge grin and endless enthusiasm. Her training sessions are intense, filled with sprint drills and powerful starts that push your body to its limits. Even when you're exhausted, she keeps cheering you on with unstoppable energy. Training beside her makes every session feel exciting and competitive.

Through relentless sprint training, your acceleration begins improving dramatically. Soon you’re able to burst ahead at the start of races before others can react. Coaches and racers start noticing how quickly you move down the track. Your speed becomes one of your greatest strengths.

Instead of focusing only on speed, you spend hours building endurance and breathing control. Long distance runs strengthen your legs and allow you to maintain pace far longer than before. Even during exhausting races, you still have energy when others begin to slow down. Your stamina becomes a powerful weapon on the track.

Super Creek trains with a calm, unreadable demeanor, teaching that racing is about control, patience, and understanding your limits—not just speed. Though her methods feel slow at first, you begin to realize you’re becoming smarter and more precise. One day, she stops you and quietly says that the path you choose will define the kind of runner you become.

You hesitate, unsure if her slow and controlled approach is really the best way forward. When you question her, Super Creek doesn’t get upset. Instead, she nods slightly, as if she expected this. She explains that doubt is natural—but rushing blindly leads to failure. She gives you two options: fully commit to her philosophy… or try combining her methods with your own aggressive style.

You decide not to fully commit, blending her patience with your natural aggression. At first, it feels powerful—you can switch between control and bursts of speed. But as training continues, cracks begin to show. Your timing becomes inconsistent, and your energy management isn’t as clean as it should be. Super Creek watches silently, eventually telling you: “Half-measures lead to unstable results.”

Realizing your mistake, you return to basics. This time, you commit fully to control and discipline. The process is slower than before, but your foundation becomes far stronger. Every step, every breath, every movement begins to feel intentional.

Weeks pass as you train under Super Creek’s guidance. Each day is structured, deliberate, and mentally demanding. You’re not just running—you’re learning how to read races, predict outcomes, and control your body with precision. Eventually, she approaches you with a new set of training options.

Instead of physical training, Super Creek has you sit and observe races. She teaches you how to analyze movement, predict pacing, and identify weaknesses in opponents. At first it feels boring—but over time, your understanding sharpens. You begin seeing patterns others can’t.

You choose to rest, allowing your body to recover. While it feels good at first, you can’t shake the feeling that you’re falling behind. When you return, others seem sharper… faster. Super Creek simply says, “Rest is important—but timing matters.”

The pool training pushes you beyond your limits. Every movement is resisted, every breath controlled. You struggle at first—but slowly adapt, building incredible endurance and discipline.

You push beyond what even Super Creek recommends. At first, it feels like rapid improvement—but your body begins to break down. Fatigue builds, your performance drops, and injuries start to creep in. Super Creek looks disappointed. “Strength without restraint leads to collapse.”

When the race begins, everything you trained for finally comes together. Your pacing is perfect and your movements feel effortless. One by one, your competitors fall behind as you surge ahead. By the finish line, your victory is undeniable.

TThe stadium roars as the final race of the season begins. Every racer here has fought hard to reach this moment. The pressure is overwhelming, but so is the excitement. Everything you have trained for leads to this one race.

You combine inspiration, friendship and discipline to become a legend.
